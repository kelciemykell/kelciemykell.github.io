The River of No Return

The Salmon river is nestled in the heart of the Idaho wilderness and runs through beautiful canyons that reach almost 5,000 ft at their deepest points. These beautful canyons boast all types of minerals and wildlife. The Salmon river wasconsidered sacred by the ancient, native tribes because of its abundant fish and resources The river earned the name "the river of no retuen" because of its strong current, which made it impossible to make a return trip after sailing downstream. Early settlers would build boats or rafts to transport themselves and goods downtream only to have to disassemble them and sell them for lumber at the end of their journey.
